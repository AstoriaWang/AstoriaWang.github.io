Public Perception

This section explores how public perception of migrant children is shaped and reflected through social media data. By analyzing comments and posts related to migrant children, we gain insights into societal attitudes and common misconceptions. This understanding can help in designing better support systems for migrant children.

Role of Social Media

Connection to Hometowns

Social media serves as a vital tool for migrant children to maintain connections with their hometowns. Platforms like Xiaohongshu, Douyin, and Kuaishou enable them to bridge the physical distances and sustain emotional bonds with their roots.

Personal Expression

Migrant children use social media for personal expression and emotional sharing. By posting personal works and sharing experiences, they can express their emotions and creativity, which is crucial for their mental well-being.

Social Interaction

Through social media, migrant children engage in social interactions, making comments, responding to others, and forming online communities. These interactions help them build social skills and feel less isolated in their new environments.

Public Misconceptions

Left-behind Children

A common misconception is that migrant children are "left-behind children" who lack parental care. However, most migrant children move with their parents and receive sufficient support and care, challenging this societal bias.

Emotional Bonds

Despite moving frequently, migrant children often maintain strong emotional bonds with their families. This continuity in family relationships is crucial for their mental health and social adaptation.

Summary

Public perception of migrant children is often shaped by misconceptions and limited understanding. By leveraging social media data, we can gain a clearer picture of their lives and challenges. This insight is crucial for developing effective support systems and policies that address their unique needs and help them thrive in urban environments.

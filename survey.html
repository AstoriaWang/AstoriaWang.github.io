Survey

This section presents the process, data, and results of our survey on migrant children. By understanding their living conditions and psychological adaptation patterns in new environments, we aim to offer a comprehensive insight into their lives and challenges.

Survey Process

Data Collection

We conducted a mixed-method research design combining quantitative and qualitative methods. The subjects were 29 migrant children living in Beijing, originating from various parts of China. Data were collected through online questionnaires covering aspects such as hometown, duration of schooling in Beijing, and social media use. Additionally, semi-structured interviews and social media behavior analysis were used to deeply explore children's emotional attitudes and social habits.

Participants

The study subjects were 29 migrant children living in Beijing who entered private migrant schools primarily around the age of 11. These children come from diverse geographical backgrounds, such as Loudi in Hunan and Qitaihe in Heilongjiang, providing rich perspectives for understanding how they adapt to different cultural and social contexts.

Survey Data

Social Media Usage

Out of 37 survey respondents, 24 are users of social media platforms like Xiaohongshu, Douyin, and Kuaishou. While the majority use social media for entertainment, a significant portion also use it for educational purposes, socializing, and maintaining contact with their hometowns.

Family Care

Survey results indicate that 25 respondents believe the parental accompaniment they receive is sufficient, with 11 considering it very sufficient and only one respondent finding it insufficient. This challenges the common societal assumption that migrant children lack family care.

Survey Results

Emotional Expression

The survey results show that many respondents do more than just browse content on social media platforms. They actively engage by leaving comments, responding to others, and posting their own content. This interaction makes social media a vital tool for personal expression and emotional sharing.

Maintaining Connections

Nearly half of the respondents use social media to maintain contact with their hometowns by browsing information about them. This implies that for migrant children, social media serves as an essential tool for connecting with their roots and managing the emotional challenges of settling in new environments.

Summary

The survey results provide valuable insights into the lives of migrant children. Despite common misconceptions, these children often receive sufficient family care and use social media as a tool for maintaining connections with their hometowns and expressing themselves. Understanding these dynamics is crucial for developing effective support systems and policies to help them thrive in urban environments.
